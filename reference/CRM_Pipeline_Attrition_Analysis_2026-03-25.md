## Key Findings

### 1. Corporate Decisions Are the #1 Reason We Lose Deals (18.8%)
The single largest coded reason. These are situations where the local facility said yes (or was interested) but the corporate parent overruled, chose a preferred vendor, or blocked us. Examples:
- Saber CMO Dr. Istenez decided no opportunity existed (Autumn Care of Madison)
- NCR corporate had concerns about "another robust NP group" (3 Chillicothe buildings)
- Lionstone/EST management companies choosing preferred providers (Oak Grove Manor, Bradford Square, Greens at Pinehurst)
- Westminster-Canterbury, English Meadows, Rose Hill — all corporate-level blocks in Virginia

**Implication:** Corporate relationship strategy matters more than facility-level selling in many cases. When corporate says no, the local champion can't override it.

### 2. Gone Dark / No Response Is a Major Leak (10.6%)
9 items where the AE had active conversations, sometimes even contracts sent, and the facility simply stopped responding. These are not flat no's — they're stalled deals with potential.
- Brickyard Healthcare — contract sent, admin reviewing "other options," 5 follow-up emails
- Homeplace Henderson (2 buildings) — CEO/Admin went dark on pending contract
- Tri-Cities Nursing — contract revisions sent, then silence
- Cedarhurst Senior Living Owensboro — multiple calls/emails to ED, no callback

**Implication:** These are the most recyclable. Adam Sabie suggests 6-month auto-recycle to New Leads. The relationship existed, the conversation happened — it just stalled.

### 3. "Not Interested" Is Broad but Real (11.8%)
10 items where the facility explicitly said no, not because of a competitor or corporate decision, but simply no perceived need. Examples:
- "Not interested in services at this time" (MajesticCare, Wyndmoor, Harpers Home)
- "Happy with current providers" (Cedar Creek of Franklin, Wyndmoor)
- Sevita — model mismatch, "not a good fit"

**Implication:** These are longer-cycle revisits. Pain points can emerge when leadership changes, incumbent fails, or facility circumstances shift. 12-month recycle per Adam's suggestion.

### 4. Incumbent Saved Business (8.2%)
7 items where the current provider got a grace period and improved — or the facility chose to stay with what they know. Examples:
- Liberty Commons — incumbent improved to 6 days/week coverage after Eventus pressure
- Ben Hur Health — gave new ownership (Proactive) a chance
- Terra Bella Southport — "happy with current provider"
- Wellington Health and Rehab — "staying with current provider"

**Implication:** Eventus created enough competitive pressure to force incumbents to improve. This is a lagging indicator of market positioning — we're moving the needle even when we don't win.

### 5. Competitor Losses (8.2% combined)
- Hospital-based provider groups: 4 items (Sanctuary at Ohio Valley, EST-Campbellsville, Charlottesville Health and Rehab, Monroe Health and Rehab)
- National/regional competitors: 3 items (McGivney → Rounding, Auburn → Ultra Health, Hillcrest Raleigh → U Health)

**Implication:** Ultra Health and hospital-affiliated groups are the most common competitive displacement. Worth tracking which competitors we lose to.

### 6. Eventus Capacity Gaps (3.5%)
3 items lost because Eventus couldn't staff coverage or had too long a wait time (Brookdale Danville Piedmont, Waynesboro Manor, Ashland Nursing and Rehab).

**Implication:** These are deals we won on merit but lost on operations. Different from a sales loss — this is a delivery gap. Waynesboro Manor had both capacity AND corporate decision factors.

### 7. Permanent Losses (4.8%)
- 2 Facility Closed / Lost License (Heritage of Sugar Mountain, Tranquility Nursing)
- 2 Inability to Meet Facility Demands (Especially Kidz — pediatric, Cedarcreek Bedford — too small)

**Implication:** These will never recycle. Filter out of any revisit pipeline.

### 8. Unknown (14.1%)
12 items with insufficient intel to code a specific reason. Includes:
- 4 zero-intel items (no comments, no AE assigned)
- 5 orphaned NC "(copy)" items — likely need territory reassignment
- 3 items that may still be alive (Five Star of Kokomo, Cedar Creek of Logansport, EST-Edgewood Manor)
