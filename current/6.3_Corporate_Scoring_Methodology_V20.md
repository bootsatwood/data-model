## 3. Scoring Model

### 3.1 Six Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Multiplier | Max Points |
|-----------|--------|------------|------------|
| Enterprise Reach | 20% | ×4 | 20 |
| Integration Readiness | 15% | ×3 | 15 |
| Strategic Influence | 15% | ×3 | 15 |
| Revenue Potential | 20% | ×4 | 20 |
| Relationship Strength | 15% | ×3 | 15 |
| AI/Tech Adoption | 15% | ×3 | 15 |
| **Total** | **100%** | | **100** |

### 3.2 Scoring Rubric (1-5 Scale)

| Score | Enterprise Reach | Integration Readiness | Strategic Influence | Revenue Potential | Relationship Strength | AI/Tech Adoption |
|-------|------------------|----------------------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| 1 | 1-4 facilities | Single service; crickets or runs from engagement | No affiliations | <$250K | At-risk/never responds | No data sharing; resistant |
| 2 | 5-9 facilities | MH only or PCP only; listens but never moves the ball forward ("false hope") | Minimal local | $250-499K | Local, no corp alignment | Limited; willing not equipped |
| 3 | 10-19 fac / 1-2 states | MH only; responsive to integration discussion but timeline expanded, not a quick sale ("slowly kick the ball down the aisle") | Regional reputation | $500-999K | Responsive when prompted | Periodic; exploring analytics |
| 4 | 20-39 facilities | MH+PCP engaged; identifying continual opportunities to support the partnership ("kicking the ball faster") | Payer/ACO connected | $1.0-1.99M | Collaborative, open | Active data sharing; dashboards |
| 5 | 40+ fac / 5+ states | Fully integrated; cross-sale opportunities, all service lines, open to everything ("they have the Eventus Bible") | REIT/ISNP/Enterprise | >$2M | Embedded partnership | Full data partnership; co-dev |

### 3.2.1 Integration Readiness — Expanded Definitions

> [!info] **Clarified by Brooke Ritchie, March 5, 2026.** These definitions resolve the PCP-only gap identified in Section 12.3.

| Score | Service Config | Behavioral Signal | What It Looks Like |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **1** | Single service line | Avoidance | We try to engage — they give us crickets or run. Very little opportunity right now. |
| **2** | MH only **or PCP only** | Passive / false hope | They'll listen to you, but never engage and move the ball forward. They give you false hope. No forward momentum. |
| **3** | MH only (or PCP only) | Responsive but slow | Responsive to the integration discussion, but they have other priorities and the timeline is expanded. Not a quick sale. We can slowly kick the ball down the aisle. |
| **4** | MH + PCP | Engaged and proactive | Both service lines active. They are engaged and identifying continual opportunities to help support the partnership. Kicking the ball faster. |
| **5** | Fully integrated (MH + PCP + MD) | Evangelized | Cross-sale opportunities, fully integrated buildings, open to all service lines. They have the Eventus Bible. |

**Key clarification:** The rubric scores on **behavioral engagement**, not just service configuration. A PCP-only entity that listens but doesn't act scores the same as an MH-only entity with the same behavior (Score 2). The differentiator between 2 and 3 is **responsiveness to integration discussion and forward momentum**, not which service line is active.

**Kisco example (Mar 2026):** Kisco has MH and is adding PCP. Tom confirmed engagement is "one-off conversations" at individual facilities, not corporate-level leadership dialogue. This combination of MH+PCP service configuration but facility-level-only engagement suggests a Score 4 (service config) tempered by Relationship Strength considerations (Score 2–3 range given lack of corporate dialogue).

### 3.3 Operational Footprint Constraint

> [!warning] **Critical Rule: Enterprise Reach and Revenue Potential are scoped to the operational footprint only.**

**Enterprise Reach:** Facility counts used for the 1–5 rubric must only include facilities within Eventus's operational footprint. Facilities in adjacent states or priority expansion states that Eventus does not currently operate in are excluded from the count.

**Revenue Potential:** The dollar value is the combination of:
- **Integration potential** — upsell opportunity at currently served facilities
- **New business (new door)** — unserved facilities at existing corporate partners
- **New business (new logo)** — facilities at new corporate relationships

All three components must be calculated from facilities within the operational footprint only. Revenue from adjacent or priority expansion states is excluded from the scoring calculation.

**Why this matters:** An operator with 200 national facilities but only 15 in our footprint scores on the 15, not the 200. This keeps scoring grounded in actionable opportunity rather than total corporate size.

### 3.4 Total Score Calculation

```
Total Score = (Enterprise Reach × 4) + (Integration Readiness × 3) + 
              (Strategic Influence × 3) + (Revenue Potential × 4) + 
              (Relationship Strength × 3) + (AI/Tech Adoption × 3)
```

## 4. Tier Definitions

| Tier | Name | Score Range | Facility Points | Count | Resource Allocation |
|------|------|-------------|-----------------|-------|---------------------|
| **T1** | Strategic Enterprise | 50-100 | 7 | 22 | 60% |
| **T2** | Growth & Expansion | 25-49 | 5 | 36 | 30% |
| **T3** | Retention/Watch | <25 | 4 | 12 | 10% |
| **T4** | Independents | Not scored | 2 | — | Facility-level engagement only |
| **T5** | Barriers | Not scored | 1 | — | Monitor; no active pursuit |

### 4.1 Facility Points

Each corporate tier contributes points to the facility-level A/B/C scoring. A facility's corporate parent tier determines how many points the "Corporate Tier" dimension adds to that facility's total score:

| Tier | Points | Rationale |
|------|--------|-----------|
| T1 | 7 | Highest-value corporate relationships amplify facility priority |
| T2 | 5 | Growth-stage corporates add meaningful facility value |
| T3 | 4 | Retention accounts still carry corporate weight |
| T4 | 2 | Independent facilities get minimal corporate uplift |
| T5 | 1 | Barriered entities contribute least to facility prioritization |

### 4.2 Tier Descriptions

- **T1–T3:** Scored MUOs. The 6-dimension scoring model (Section 3) determines placement.
- **T4 — Independents:** Non-MUO entities that do not meet the 7+ facility or metro presence criteria. Engaged at the facility level rather than through corporate account management.
- **T5 — Barriers:** Entities where structural barriers prevent growth (e.g., own provider group, exclusive alliance, CON restrictions). Current revenue is defended but no active pursuit of new business.

### 4.2 Engagement Models by Tier

| Tier | Executive Access | Account Management | Review Cadence |
|------|------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| T1 | C-suite | Dedicated CAM | Quarterly exec reviews |
| T2 | Regional leadership | CAM-led, rotational clinical | Regular QBRs |
| T3 | Facility level | Field-managed | Annual touchpoints |
| T4 | None (facility-level) | Field-managed | As needed |
| T5 | Monitor only | Defend current revenue | Annual review of barrier status |

## 5. MUO Criteria

### 5.1 Multi-Unit Operator Definition

An entity qualifies as an MUO if it meets BOTH criteria:
1. **7+ facilities** nationally
2. **At least 1 facility** in a metro market (12 priority metros)

### 5.2 Criteria Application

| Criterion | Book1_Scoring (Original) | MUO Framework |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------|
| Facility Count | >7 required | 7+ required |
| Metro Presence | **NOT required** | **Required** |

**Key Finding:** Book1_Scoring included entities with 7+ facilities regardless of metro presence. The stricter MUO definition requires both.

### 12.3 ~~Open Questions~~ RESOLVED — Integration Readiness Rubric Gaps

> [!success] **Resolved March 5, 2026.** Brooke clarified that Integration Readiness scores on **behavioral engagement**, not service configuration alone. See Section 3.2.1 for expanded definitions.

**Resolution summary:**

| Original Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Where does "PCP only" fall on the 1–5 scale? | **Score 2 or 3** — same as MH only. PCP-only is explicitly included at Score 2. The differentiator between 2 and 3 is behavioral (passive vs. responsive to integration discussion), not which service line is active. |
| Is PCP-only adding MH the same trajectory as MH-only adding PCP? | **Yes.** Both paths lead to Score 4 (MH+PCP engaged). The rubric is service-line-agnostic at the single-service level. |
| Should the rubric score on number of service lines rather than which specific line? | **Partially.** Service configuration sets the floor (single-line caps at 3, dual-line starts at 4, fully integrated = 5), but behavioral engagement determines the exact score within that range. |

Previously unresolved scenarios are now clear:

| Scenario | Current State | Action | Resulting State | Score |
|----------|---------------|--------|-----------------|-----------------------|
| A | No service | Add MH | MH only | 2 (passive) or 3 (responsive) |
| B | MH only | Add PCP | Integrated (MH+PCP) | 4 (engaged) |
| C | **PCP only** | — | PCP only | **2 (passive) or 3 (responsive)** |
| D | **PCP only** | Add MH | Integrated (MH+PCP) | **4 (engaged)** |
| E | MH only | Deepen MH | Still MH only | 2 or 3 (depends on behavioral signal) |

### 12.4 Open Question — Portfolio Alignment as a Scoring Dimension

> [!warning] **Should percentage of portfolio penetration be a factor in scoring?**

**Example scenario:**
- 10 facilities, 2 service lines each = 20 total service slots
- We serve 18 of 20 (90% portfolio alignment)
- An AE sells the final 2 slots → 100% alignment

**Questions to resolve:**
1. Does completing the last 10% of a portfolio have the same value as winning the first 10%? The revenue is the same, but the effort and strategic significance differ.
2. Is there a "diminishing returns" dynamic where high-penetration accounts generate less incremental value per sale? Or is there an "increasing returns" argument where full alignment creates stickier partnerships, stronger outcomes data, and better retention?
3. Should portfolio alignment (% of total service slots filled) be tracked as a metric even if it doesn't directly feed the 1–5 score? It could inform Relationship Strength or Integration Readiness indirectly.
4. How does this interact with the AE compensation model? If completing a portfolio is undervalued in scoring, does that create a misalignment with field incentives?
