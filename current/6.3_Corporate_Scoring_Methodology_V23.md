## 1. Purpose

This document describes the V23 corporate scoring methodology for the Multi-Unit Operator (MUO) tiering framework. V23 replaces the V20 qualitative-only approach (Brooke's Book1_Scoring.xlsx) with a **data-driven methodology** where 5 of 6 dimensions are computed from database fields, Finance workbook notes, and structured web research.

| Aspect | V20 | V23 |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Scoring approach** | All 6 dimensions qualitative (Brooke) | 5 data-driven, 1 qualitative (AI only) |
| **Universe** | 70 entities (national) | 60 Finance-tracked, 38 scored (footprint-gated + bed/address rules) |
| **Footprint** | 14 states (incorrect) | 6 states: NC, SC, VA, KY, OH, IN |
| **Campus counting** | Raw facility rows | Street-number matching: (street_number, city), >15 beds only |

| **IR source** | Brooke behavioral | DB service flags (MH_Flag, PCP_Flag, Integrated_Flag) |
| **SI source** | Brooke behavioral | Tom's Notes + web research (REIT/ISNP/ACO) |

## 3. Scoring Universe

### 3.1 Finance 60 Entity Disposition

Finance tracks 60 corporate entities in the MUO Data workbook. Each is classified as follows:

| Disposition | Count | Rule |
|-------------|-------|------|
| **Scored (T1/T2/T3)** | 38 | ≥7 qualifying campuses in footprint, no barrier |
| **T4 — Independent** | 12 | <7 qualifying campuses in footprint |
| **T5 — Hard Barrier** | 10 | Structural barrier prevents growth |
| **Total** | 60 | |

A "qualifying campus" is a unique (street_number, city) pair with >15 Total_Beds. See Sections 3.3 and 3.4.

### 3.2 Operational Footprint

**6 states:** NC, SC, VA, KY, OH, IN

All dimensions (ER, RP, RS, IR) are scoped to facilities within these states only. An operator with 200 national facilities but only 15 in our footprint scores on the 15.

### 3.3 MUO Gate Rule

An entity qualifies as an MUO if it has **≥7 qualifying campuses** within the operational footprint.

- **7 or more qualifying campuses** → scored as MUO (T1/T2/T3)
- **6 or fewer qualifying campuses** → **T4 Independent** (not scored, facility-level engagement)
- This is a hard gate, not a tiebreaker

**Campus definition (V8 — Street-Number Matching):** A campus is a unique **(street_number, city)** pair within the same entity. If an address has no leading street number, falls back to (normalized_address, city). Co-located SNF + ALF at the same street number and city = 1 campus.

This approach handles:
- **Abbreviation differences:** "12019 Verhoeff Dr" and "12019 VERHOEFF DRIVE" → same campus (both street number 12019 in same city)
- **Suffix variations:** "625 Bethany Rd" and "625 Bethany Church Road" → same campus
- **Direction spelling:** "509 N Hayden Ave" and "509 North Hayden Avenue" → same campus

Standard address normalization (St/Street, Dr/Drive, etc.) is also applied as a fallback for addresses without leading numbers.

### 3.4 Bed Minimum Rule (V8)

**Facility rows with Total_Beds ≤ 15 are excluded from campus counting (ER) and revenue summation (RP).**

These rows still appear in the workbook detail tabs for transparency, but they do not contribute to the entity's campus count or total revenue.

**Why this rule exists:**

The V23 database contains facility rows that represent Independent Living (IL), Memory Care (MC) cottages, and small satellite units that are not addressable clinical service opportunities. Examples discovered during V7 analysis:

| Facility | Entity | Beds | Type |
|----------|--------|------|------|
| Runk & Pratt Liberty Ridge-IL | Runk & Pratt | 3 | Independent Living |
| The Summit-IL | LifeSpire of Virginia | 13 | Independent Living |
| Brookdale Carriage Club II ALZ | Brookdale Senior Living | 7 | Memory Care / Alzheimer's |
| The Cardinal at North Hills MC | Kisco Senior Living | 11 | Memory Care |
| Carolina Reserve of Durham MC | Navion | 9 | Memory Care |
| Grand Victorian of Greenwood | StoryPoint | 4 | Small ALF cottage |

Without this rule, these rows inflate campus counts (ER) and can push entities across the 7-campus MUO gate when they shouldn't qualify. The 15-bed threshold was chosen because:

1. **It catches the right things.** All explicitly labeled IL and MC rows in the DB are ≤15 beds. Small ALF satellite cottages (4–14 beds) are also captured.
2. **It doesn't disrupt legitimate facilities.** The smallest legitimate SNF in the DB is ~18 beds. The smallest legitimate ALF operations are in the 20–30 bed range. No facility that represents a real clinical service opportunity is excluded.
3. **The impact is surgical.** Across all 50 Finance entities in footprint, only 43 rows (out of ~2,000+) are excluded. Only 1 entity changes tier (Runk & Pratt, T3→T4). 4 entities see minor score adjustments within their existing tier.

**Optics rationale:** When presenting the scoring model to leadership, every campus and every revenue dollar in the model should represent a real, addressable clinical opportunity. Including 3-bed IL cottages or 7-bed MC wings alongside 120-bed SNFs undermines the credibility of the analysis. The bed minimum ensures the numbers we present can withstand scrutiny.

## 4. Scoring Model

### 4.1 Formula

```
Total Score = (ER × 4) + (IR × 3) + (SI × 3) + (RP × 4) + (RS × 3) + (AI × 3)
Maximum possible = 100
```

### 4.2 Six Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Abbrev | Weight | Multiplier | Max | V23 Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|------------|-----|------------|
| Enterprise Reach | ER | 20% | ×4 | 20 | Data-driven: campus count in FP |
| Integration Readiness | IR | 15% | ×3 | 15 | Data-driven: DB service flags |
| Strategic Influence | SI | 15% | ×3 | 15 | Data-driven: Tom's Notes + web research |
| Revenue Potential | RP | 20% | ×4 | 20 | Data-driven: S2 Total Potential Revenue |
| Relationship Strength | RS | 15% | ×3 | 15 | Data-driven: Tom's Shared Savings Notes |
| AI/Tech Adoption | AI | 15% | ×3 | 15 | Qualitative: V20 Brooke scores (31 of 47), default=0 |

## 5. Dimension Definitions

### 5.1 Enterprise Reach (ER) — Weight ×4

**Method:** Count unique qualifying campuses (street-number collapsed, >15 beds) within the 6-state operational footprint. See Sections 3.3 and 3.4.

**Brackets (Option C):**

| Score | Campus Count | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | <9 campuses | Small footprint |
| 2 | 9–12 campuses | Moderate presence |
| 3 | 13–19 campuses | Significant regional |
| 4 | 20–39 campuses | Multi-state enterprise |
| 5 | 40+ campuses | Large-scale operator |

**Why Option C:** Tested against Options A (10/15/25/50) and V20 Original (5/10/20/40). Option C produces the most even distribution across the Finance scoreable universe (roughly equal counts per bracket).

**V20 comparison:** V20 brackets were 5/10/20/40 and counted raw facility rows nationally. V23 counts unique campuses in footprint only, so the brackets were adjusted upward to compensate for the smaller counting base.

### 5.2 Integration Readiness (IR) — Weight ×3

**Method:** Formulaic for ALL entities from V23 DB service flags (MH_Flag, PCP_Flag, Integrated_Flag). Computed per campus.

**V20 used Brooke's qualitative behavioral scores.** V23 replaces with a deterministic approach based on service configuration at the campus level.

| Score | Service Configuration | Logic |
|-------|----------------------|-------|
| 1 | Not served | No Do_We_Serve=YES rows in footprint |
| 2 | Single service only | MH only OR PCP only at served campuses |
| 3 | Both MH + PCP at separate campuses | MH campuses and PCP campuses exist but don't overlap |
| 4 | Dual MH+PCP at same campus, or 25–49% integrated | At least one campus has both MH_Flag + PCP_Flag, or Integrated_Flag on 25–49% of campuses |
| 5 | Majority integrated | Integrated_Flag on ≥50% of campuses |

**Key rule:** Service configuration sets the **floor**. The data-driven approach cannot capture behavioral engagement signals (e.g., "they have the Eventus Bible") that V20's qualitative scoring reflected. This is an accepted trade-off for reproducibility.

**Brackets (Option B):**

| Score | S2 Total Potential Revenue |
|-------|---------------------------|
| 1 | <$1M |
| 2 | $1M – $2.5M |
| 3 | $2.5M – $5M |
| 4 | $5M – $10M |
| 5 | >$10M |

**Why Option B:** V20 brackets ($250K/$500K/$1M/$2M) were calibrated for consent-based revenue. S2 TAM is much larger — every entity with 7+ campuses exceeds $2M. Option B ($1M/$2.5M/$5M/$10M) produces the best spread across the Finance scoreable universe (roughly 12/11/11/10/13 distribution).

### 6.2 Why Thresholds Changed

V23's data-driven methodology produces higher average scores than V20's qualitative approach because:
- ER and RP brackets are calibrated to the footprint-gated universe (most entities get ER ≥2 and RP ≥2)
- Tom's RS notes provide more generous scoring than Brooke's behavioral assessments for some entities
- IR from service flags has a different distribution than Brooke's behavioral IR

At the original 50/25 thresholds, V5 (pre-threshold change) produced T1=26, T2=18, T3=1 — essentially no T3 tier. Raising to 55/35 creates a meaningful T3 population. With V8's street-number matching and bed minimum, the final distribution is T1=19, T2=17, T3=2 (38 scored).

## 9. Address Normalization — Street-Number Matching (V8)

V8 resolves the address abbreviation inflation problem that required manual overrides in V7.

### 9.1 The Problem (V7 and earlier)

The V7 campus-collapsing method used raw (Address, City) pairs. This failed when ALF and SNF rows at the same physical location used different address formats:

| ALF Address | SNF Address | Same Location? |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| 12019 Verhoeff Dr | 12019 VERHOEFF DRIVE | Yes — but V7 counted as 2 campuses |
| 625 Bethany Rd | 625 Bethany Church Road | Yes — but V7 counted as 2 campuses |
| 501 Oak Ave. | 501 OAK AVENUE | Yes — but V7 counted as 2 campuses |

This inflated campus counts, causing entities to cross the MUO gate incorrectly. Atrium Health had 13 raw campus keys for 7 actual locations; Brighton had 8 for 4.

### 9.2 The Solution (V8)

**Street-number matching** uses the leading street number + city as the campus key: `(street_number, city)`. Two addresses at the same street number in the same city, for the same entity, are treated as one campus regardless of how the street name is spelled.

This catches all the cases that standard abbreviation normalization (St/Street, Dr/Drive, etc.) misses — including "Bethany Rd" vs "Bethany Church Rd" (different words, not just abbreviation).

**Entities affected by the change from V7 → V8:**

| Entity | V7 Campuses | V8 Campuses | Change | V7 Tier | V8 Tier |
|--------|------------|------------|--------|---------|---------|
| Atrium Health | 7 (after bed rule) | 6 | -1 | T3 | T4 |
| BHI Senior Living | 8 | 6 | -2 | T3 | T4 |
| Brighton | 8 | 4 | -4 | T3 | T4 |
| Kissito Healthcare | 7 | 6 | -1 | T3 | T4 |
| Sunnyside Communities | 6 | 3 | -3 | T4 | T4 |
| Caring Place Healthcare | 6 | 5 | -1 | T4 | T4 |
| American Healthcare LLC | 7 | 6 | -1 | scored | T4 |
| LifeSpire of Virginia | 7 | 6 | -1 | scored | T4 |
| Lutheran Life Villages | 7 | 6 | -1 | scored | T4 |

**Accepted risk:** In rare cases, two genuinely different addresses at the same street number in the same city could be falsely collapsed (e.g., "836 Hospital Dr" and "836 Ray Leggett Way" in New Bern for Pruitt Health). This has no scoring impact for Pruitt (still well above the gate) and is acceptable given the accuracy improvement overall.
