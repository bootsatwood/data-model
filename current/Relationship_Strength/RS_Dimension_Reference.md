# Relationship Strength (RS) — Dimension Reference

**Last updated:** 2026-03-12
**Weight in model:** x3 (15% of total score, max 15 points)
**Production source:** Tom's Shared Savings engagement scores (V23)
**Tracker:** [Corporate Relationship Tracker](https://onsitecare.monday.com/boards/18403704088) (Monday.com)

---

## 1. What RS Measures

Relationship Strength captures how deep and active our engagement is with a corporate entity at the **corporate leadership level** — not facility-level one-offs.

Two lenses exist, each measuring a different aspect:

| Lens | Assessor | What It Captures | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **BD Engagement** | Brooke | Behavioral momentum — is the entity moving toward partnership? | Forward-looking, names and timelines | Snapshot from Dec 2025, aging |
| **SS Pipeline** | Tom | Shared savings reality — are they on paper or actively engaged? | Ground truth on current state | Blind spots on entities Tom hasn't met |

**Neither lens is complete.** Both left ~20 of ~70 entities at default/unknown. Different 20 in many cases.

## 4. Tier Impact — Does RS Actually Move the Needle?

### If Brooke's RS Replaced Tom's RS in V23

| Tier Shifts | Count |
|---|---|
| Total | **1** |

| Entity | Current Tier | Current Score | New Score | New Tier |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TerraBella Senior Living | T2 | 52 | 55 | **T1** |

That's it. One entity, right on the T1/T2 boundary.

### Why So Little Movement?

RS is weighted x3 (15% of max 100). Even a 4-point RS swing (Otterbein: 1 -> 5) only moves the total score by 12 points. The other five dimensions — Enterprise Reach (x4), Integration Readiness (x3), Strategic Influence (x3), Revenue Potential (x4), AI/Tech Adoption (x3) — carry enough combined weight to stabilize tiers.

**The model is robust to RS disagreement.** This is good news for the tracker approach — it means we can iterate on RS scores without destabilizing the tier system.

## 6. Decision: Living Relationship Tracker (Option E)

Rather than a one-time swap between Brooke and Tom's snapshots, RS is now a **living dual-perspective tracker**.

**Structure:**
- Monday.com board: [Corporate Relationship Tracker](https://onsitecare.monday.com/boards/18403704088)
- Columns: RS-Tom (SS), Tom Notes, RS-Brooke (BD), Brooke Notes, RS-Production, Coverage status
- 56 entities populated with both assessors' current data

**How RS-Production works today:** = Tom's score (status quo)

**How it should evolve:**
- Tom's score remains authoritative for SS engagement
- Brooke's score informs BD prioritization and fills Tom's blind spots
- Coverage gaps (gray status) are visible and can be triaged
- Periodic refresh cycle (quarterly?) keeps scores current
- As Tom's coverage grows, the tracker shows convergence naturally

**What to improve for V24:**
- [ ] Harmonize the two scales — Brooke and Tom are using different 1-5 rubrics for different things. Consider a unified RS rubric that captures both BD engagement and SS pipeline.
- [ ] Fill the gap entities — 11 entities still at RS=1 from both assessors. These need targeted outreach.
- [ ] Add Ian's perspective — Tom deferred to "Brooke and Ian" on 3 entities. Ian's field intel is a third lens not yet captured.
- [ ] Automate RS-Production derivation — currently manual. Could be max(Tom, Brooke) or a weighted formula.
- [ ] Track trajectory — the tracker captures snapshots but not direction. Consider adding a "Trend" column (warming/cooling/stable).
- [ ] Connect to CRM Pipeline — board relation to Corporate Scoring Reference so RS changes auto-propagate.
